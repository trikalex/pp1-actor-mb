Marius Bodochi

About

    Biography


  • Marius Bodochi is a famous Romanian film and theatre actor born on September 6, 1959 in Cluj Napoca, Romania.

  • Marius Bodochi graduated from the Theater Institute in Târgu Mureș , Professor Constantin Codrescu 's class, class of 1982.

  • Between 1991 and 2000 he was a year teacher, university lecturer at "Babeș Bolyai" University Cluj, Faculty of Letters, Department of Theatre.

  • He made his debut in 1982, the last year of his studies, on the stage of the National Theater in Cluj Napoca, with the role of Christopher Flanders in "The Train Doesn't Stop Here" by Tennessee Williams.

  • He has been playing on the stage of the "ILCaragiale" National Theater in Bucharest since 2004. He made memorable creations in the roles of Constantin in "Săptamâna luminată" - directed by Mihai Măniuțiu at the Cluj National Theater, Dionyssos in "Bacantele" - directed by Mihai Măniuțiu at the National Theater in Bucharest (1997) , Bolinbroke from "Richard II" - directed by Mihai Măniuțiu at the National Theater Bucharest, Oberon from "A Midsummer Night's Dream" - directed by Victor Ioan Frunză at the National Theater Cluj, the King from "The King Dies" - directed by Victor Ioan Frunză at Theatrum Mundi 2002), Antonio Salieri from "at the Bucharest National Opera (2010).
